peerRTF: Robust MVDR Beamforming Using Graph Convolutional Network



Daniel Levi, Amit Sofer, Sharon Gannot


This paper introduces a novel method for accurately and reliably identifying the relative transfer functions (RTFs) between microphones in relation to a desired source, which is crucial for the design of microphone array beamformers. Accurate RTF estimation is particularly challenging in noisy and reverberant environments. To address this, the proposed method leverages prior knowledge of the acoustic environment to enhance the robustness of RTF estimation by learning the RTF manifold. The key innovation of this work is the use of a graph convolutional network (GCN) to learn and infer a robust representation of the RTFs within a confined area. This approach is tested and trained using real recordings, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving beamformer performance by providing a more reliable RTF estimation in complex acoustic settings.
